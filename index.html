Subtheme: Housing for the Rich

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. How have high-rise buildings reshaped the real estate market?
  2. What trends can be observed in property investment activities across different property types?
  3. How have property flips influenced the market dynamics?

Discoveries & Insights

Summary

High-rise development, investor activity, and property flipping are influencing the urban housing market. Corporations have increasingly taken ownership of high-rise buildings (with a siginificant increase in certain ZIP codes), suggesting a shift towards assets that promise substantial returns. This shift in property ownership could impact the socioeconomic makeup of communities and raise critical questions about housing affordability. Investment patterns have similarly changed over time, revealing a decline in properties acquired by non-investors. The luxury real estate segment has shown a remarkable increase in both the share of transactions and median real prices, reflecting a shift toward wealthier demographic. The practice of property flipping has seen a trend toward long/medium-term investments. Looking forward, these trends highlight the necessity for urban planning and policy-making that ensures that growth and development in Boston remain sustainable and equitable for all.
